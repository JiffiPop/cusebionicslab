Senior Design

Aerospace Engineering Senior Design Competition

In my last semester at Syracuse University, a design competition was held amongst the senior aerospace engineering students.  Both a bottle rocket filled with water and pressurized air, and a radio-controlled aircraft that carries the bottle rocket as a payload are designed by the students.  The objective of the competition was to score the most lap-seconds.  Laps are counted by how many lap the radio-controlled aircraft flies around the figure-eight course.  Seconds are counted by how many seconds the rocket is in the air for.  These individual scores are multiplied to obtain the team's final score.  Shown below are images of my team's aircraft and rocket design featuring specifically all of the work I completed.  It should be noted that the image above is the aircraft starter kit, (HBZ3100 Mini Apprentice S), each team was given to take components from.
